# Product vision

## Mission

ArmA AI Bridge will provide a believable, persistent radio connection to **Papa Bear**, an AI HQ, operations and logistics officer. The player can speak naturally, request information, ask for recommendations and later request authorized support. Papa Bear combines general reasoning with current Arma state, ACE3 state, static map knowledge and mission-defined capabilities.

## Core user experience

The player should be able to say or type:

- “What is our current situation?”
- “Which friendly unit is closest to us?”
- “Is there a settlement nearby?”
- “Find a suitable landing zone west of my position.”
- “Alpha Two has two wounded. Can you arrange an evacuation?”
- “Firing solution, range 740, bearing 062.”
- “Explain why you selected that route.”
- ordinary off-topic or explanatory questions that do not require game tools.

Papa Bear retrieves facts, distinguishes current observations from inference, asks for missing information, proposes alternatives and reports the progress of accepted operations.

## Functional pillars

### Conversation

Open-ended multilingual conversation with short in-memory continuity. Tool use is optional and selected by the reasoning layer according to the question.

### Situational awareness

A local world model represents the player, friendly forces, known enemy contacts, assets, mission objectives, requests and environmental state. Every fact has provenance and freshness.

### Static knowledge

A persistent spatial database indexes the loaded map and relevant mod configuration. It supports named locations, roads, structures, terrain, vegetation, water, infrastructure, landing-zone candidates and equipment/ballistics metadata.

### Deterministic services

Ballistics, distances, bearings, routes and landing-zone scoring are computed locally. OpenAI chooses and explains tools; it is not the numerical source of truth.

### Authorized actions

Mission authors expose a capability registry. Papa Bear may plan and request only declared actions. A local gateway validates permissions, resources and parameters before Arma receives a command.

### Voice

Push-to-talk radio interaction uses OpenAI audio transcription for speech recognition and ElevenLabs exclusively for Papa Bear's voice. Voice services are clients of the same orchestrator used by typed conversation.

## Non-goals

- No omniscient enemy tracking.
- No arbitrary SQF execution generated by a language model.
- No autonomous player input, injection or anti-cheat bypass.
- No immediate attempt to support every Arma or ACE subsystem.
- No full raw-world JSON sent to OpenAI on every telemetry frame.
- No language-model-generated ballistic estimate presented as a firing solution.

## Success criteria for v1

- Reliable normal conversation and current-state questions.
- Complete cached static map index with named-location and terrain retrieval.
- Friendly-force and known-contact world model with confidence/freshness.
- ACE capability detection and stable data adapter.
- Firing solutions matching the active ACE weapon/ammunition/weather configuration within agreed tolerances.
- At least one end-to-end support request type with observable lifecycle.
- Push-to-talk voice round trip with acceptable latency and interruption handling.
